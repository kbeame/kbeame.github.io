Science Behind the Sick

Salmonella

This foodborne illness is most often contracted from the eating or improper handling of contaminated and undercooked foods, most commonly poultry products (eggs and meat). The most common symptoms for this are bowel movement and pain as well as severe dehydration.

Listeria

Listeria is a serious infection caused by the bacterium Listeria monocytogenes. It primarily affects older adults, pregnant women, newborns, and adults with weakened immune systems.

Unlike many other germs, Listeria can grow even in the cold temperature of a refrigerator. Listeria is killed by cooking and pasteurization.
